LifeChain AI
Current Project Summary

LifeChain AI is a healthcare records and clinical intelligence platform built to unify patient data,
clinical workflows, laboratory reporting, family-history context, and AI-assisted disease tracking
in one system.

This repository contains the active implementation of the platform rather than the original FYP
proposal draft. The codebase is split into a FastAPI backend, a Next.js frontend, model training
utilities, trained model artifacts, and supporting documentation.

Project goals

- Maintain a single longitudinal patient record across visits, reports, diagnoses, prescriptions,
  and disease progression history
- Support separate role-based workflows for admins, doctors, patients, and labs
- Enable chronic disease monitoring from structured lab values
- Support oral cancer image screening and progression tracking
- Link patient family relationships for hereditary context and disease-history analysis
- Provide multilingual utilities and an extensible AI-assisted healthcare workflow

Current implemented scope

1. Patient management
   - create, update, search, and view patient profiles
   - unique patient identity tied to the rest of the platform
   - family relationship graph and family disease history access

2. Doctor workflows
   - create doctor profiles
   - manage visits, symptoms, diagnoses, and prescriptions
   - review patient history and progression reports

3. Lab workflows
   - create lab profiles and reports
   - add structured test results from supported reference ranges
   - mark reports as completed to trigger downstream progression and diagnosis updates
   - upload oral scan images and store screening results

4. AI and analytics
   - multi-disease structured inference support
   - progression timeline and forecast reporting
   - oral cancer image-based detection workflow
   - model training and evaluation utilities under model_training/

5. Platform services
   - JWT authentication and role checks
   - translation endpoints
   - SMTP-based credential email delivery for newly created doctor and lab accounts

Technology stack

- Backend: FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, Alembic, PostgreSQL, Pydantic
- Frontend: Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4, Framer Motion
- AI/ML: TensorFlow, PyTorch, XGBoost, scikit-learn, Roboflow integrations
- Dev runtime: Docker Compose with separate backend and frontend containers

Operational notes

- The current source of truth is the running code, not older proposal-era documents.
- Environment variables are loaded from .env by the backend.
- Gmail SMTP requires an App Password for successful credential delivery.
- Some older docs may describe earlier APIs or proposal placeholders; verify against code when in doubt.
